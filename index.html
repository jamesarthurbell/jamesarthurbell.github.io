What is Undue Influence?

Influence is everywhere, and a lot of what we do is based on the influence of others. Much of the time influence is benign — our teachers, counselors, and parents try to influence us for our own good. We might even pay a personal trainer to push us beyond our comfort level in an exercise program. Influence that we are aware of, that we have asked for, and that benefits us can be a good thing. After all, humans are social animals and we’re usually better when working together. Influence can sometimes be surreptitious, however, and not for our own good but for the benefit of the individual or group exerting the influence. This is undue influence.
